Australian Government Department of Health
UI/UX ReDesign

As part of my UX/UI Bootcamp at Monash University, I undertook a project to assist an Australian government organization in improving their website's accessibility and navigation by enhancing user interface and information architecture. Specifically, I focused on redesigning the website for the Department of Health and Aged Care.

  • Solo work
  • User testing
  • UX Auditing
  • Information architecture

Define

I have created a user persona to understand the typical user, as well as conducted user tests that asked participants to download a poster or social tile explaining how to use a nasal swab rapid antigen test (RAT), preferably in Chinese. Through this research, I have identified some usability issues of the website.

Ideate

Upon reviewing the original website, it became apparent that users are likely to feel overwhelmed and confused by the vast array of health-related information available. As such, I conducted an information architecture test and identified several pain points. Based on these findings, I have made a new site map.

Design

Started with a UI moodboard, I want the site to be presented healthy, professional and clean. A UI Style Guide was meticulously developed. This guide outlines the design principles and visual elements that embody Department of Health, such as a color scheme that demonstrates health and wellness, and the typography that is legible.

Prototype

After several iterations of wireframes and low-fidelity prototypes, I have arrived at a polished high-fidelity prototype.
